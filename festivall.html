About Eid

Eid is a special time for Muslims around the world, marking the end of Ramadan, the holy month of fasting. It is a celebration of joy, gratitude, and togetherness.Eid, also known as Eid al-Fitr, is a significant religious festival celebrated by Muslims worldwide. It marks the end of Ramadan, the holy month of fasting, prayer, and reflection. The word "Eid" translates to "festivity" or "celebration" in Arabic, and indeed, the occasion is marked by joy, gratitude, and a sense of communal unity.

Eid festival

Eid al-Fitr, often referred to simply as Eid, signifies the completion of a month-long period of fasting from dawn to sunset during Ramadan. This fasting is one of the Five Pillars of Islam, emphasizing self-discipline, spiritual growth, and empathy for those who are less fortunate.
